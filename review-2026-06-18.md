# Review: geodesics-of-care

## Verdict
**Reject as written, with a clear path to accept-with-major-revisions at a theory/philosophy venue** (it is not an empirical paper and would be desk-rejected at an empirical Nature-level venue on those grounds alone). The framework is genuinely interesting and unusually honest about its own limits, but its single most serious problem is that the central formal claim — that care-distance is a *directed quasi-metric* — is mathematically inconsistent with the paper's own repeated claim that the distance *violates the triangle inequality*. A quasi-metric, in the standard sense the paper itself implies (a metric minus symmetry), retains the triangle inequality. The one piece of precise formalism on which the paper stakes its identity ("precise definitions that do work rather than decorate") is mislabeled. Secondarily, the shipped simulation demonstrates only the internal coherence of stipulated numbers, which §7 admits but the abstract oversells.

## Claim grounding
- "we show that this distance is a directed quasi-metric: it is asymmetric, and it can violate the triangle inequality" (Abstract; repeated §3, §7.1) → **overstated / mislabeled.** The two stated properties are mutually exclusive under the standard definition the paper invokes. See Argument.
- "care-distance is a quasi-metric; preserving the other's option-entropy has a measurable price… and that price is small while the welfare lost to coercion's failure is large" (Abstract) → **supported-but-overgeneralized, and the wording is broken.** The decimals (0.028, 0.505) are in `results.json`; the code matches. But "the welfare lost to *coercion's failure*" is the wrong phrase — coercion does not fail in the model; *abandonment* does (price of freedom = 0.505). And "the price is small" is true only for horizon 8 / start 4 / ln 2 floor; it is a property of those settings, not of care.
- "kin are an order of magnitude cheaper to reach than distant strangers" (Abstract; §7.3) → **supported as arithmetic, overstated as insight.** 11.44× is in the data and the Bernoulli-KL math checks out. But the kin/stranger/distant efficiencies (1.0/0.5/0.25) are *stipulated inputs*, not derived; the only thing the model "shows" is that KL divergence is convex near the boundary. "This is kin selection rendered as geometry" (§7.3) overstates: the kin discount is an assumption rendered as geometry.
- "the most efficient way to secure a being's viability is very often to take away its freedom" (§5) → **asserted, but well-motivated** by the paternalism/colonial/psychiatry examples and then made concrete by §7.2; acceptable.
- "an alignment requirement derived from machine learning and a demand derived from Levinas converge on the same constraint is the strongest evidence the paper offers that the autonomy floor is a structural feature of care" (§5) → **insightful but oversold as "evidence."** Convergence of two framings the author has himself mapped onto a common formalism is suggestive, not evidence; it risks circularity (both were translated into "preserve B's option set" by the author).
- "Bureaucracy… is a low-empathy and high-radius care technology" (§8) → **asserted, but as illuminating reframing rather than empirical claim**; fair given the paper's stated "organizational not empirical" scope.

## Argument
- **§3 / §7.1 — the quasi-metric mislabel (most serious).** The paper defines "A metric is symmetric and obeys the triangle inequality," then says care-distance drops *both* (it is directed *and* violates the triangle inequality) and calls the result a "directed quasi-metric." Dropping symmetry alone gives a quasi-metric; dropping the triangle inequality too gives something strictly weaker (a *premetric* / directed dissimilarity). There is a second, analysis-flavored sense of "quasi-metric" that relaxes the triangle inequality by a constant — but that sense *requires symmetry*, which this object also lacks. So neither standard definition fits, and under the definition the paper's own prose implies, the central classification is self-contradictory. This is fixable by renaming, but as written it is an error at the exact point the paper claims maximal rigor.
- **§7.2 — "option-entropy" is a weak proxy for autonomy.** In the code the entropy floor is on the distribution *the carer imposes* over B's moves; B never chooses. So "B keeps live options" is operationalized as "the carer's lottery is not too deterministic." Carer-imposed randomness is not the same thing as B's agency. The autonomy constraint, as implemented, forbids the carer from being deterministic — a strange formal residue of Noddings/Levinas/corrigibility, all of which are about the recipient's *power to refuse and redirect*, which this model contains nowhere.
- **§7.2 — the smallness of the "price of autonomy" is baked in.** At the ln 2 floor the optimizer can put ~0.5 mass on "up" and largely exclude "down," so over 8 steps it nearly reaches the top regardless. The result that freedom is cheap is therefore partly an artifact of a floor that still lets the carer foreclose the harmful option. The paper hedges ("a fact about itself"), but the abstract presents "the price is small" as a feature of care.
- **§7 generally — the model proves realizability, not the thesis.** Asymmetry is guaranteed by `actcost(A)≠actcost(B)`; triangle violation by squared `repcost`; the kin gradient by stipulated efficiencies. The model cannot *fail* to exhibit these. §7's opening sentence concedes this honestly ("a fact about itself rather than a measurement of the moral world"), but the abstract's "A minimal control model, solved exactly, makes three claims concrete" reads as evidential support it is not.
- **§1 / Abstract — the leap to "the moral world is a broken manifold."** A three-point toy with hand-chosen convex costs licenses "this structure is realizable," not "the moral world is curved and broken." The metaphysical generalization outruns the model.
- **Genuinely strong:** the layered weak/strong taxonomy (§1) with the repeatedly enforced discipline of not reading a lower layer as a higher one (rats, quorum sensing); the Levinas↔corrigibility convergence (§5/§9), which is a real and non-obvious synthesis; the topology vocabulary (moral hole / bottleneck / singularity, §4) that locates real phenomena; and §9's three honestly stated limits (phenomenological gap, incommensurability, imperialism of viability), which is more intellectual self-restraint than most papers in this genre manage.

## Factual errors
- The two citation errors above (Crimston title "circle"→"world"; Singer subtitle belongs to the 2011 edition, not the 1981 FSG one). Both verified.
- Abstract wording: "the welfare lost to coercion's failure is large" — substantively wrong/confused; the large loss (0.505) is the cost of *abandonment* (freedom/random walk), not of coercion.
- No errors found in the simulation: every headline decimal (1.2, 1.5, 0.3, 4.2, 2.7, 1.5; 1.0, 0.972, 0.495, 0.088, 0.028; 0.768, 2.554, 8.789, 11.44, 3.32) appears in `results.json`, and the prose of §7 accurately describes the model `analyses.py` implements. The Bernoulli-KL and ladder arithmetic I re-derived by hand match.
- Minor: "responsibility precedes and limits any project" (§5) leans on the *Otherwise than Being* (1974) formulation while citing *Totality and Infinity* (1961); defensible but slightly anachronistic.

## Prose and insight
**Alive:** "the suffering that is no one's path" (§4); "the padded room, optimal viability at zero agency" (§7.2); "Bureaucracy… is a low-empathy and high-radius care technology" and "strangers become obligations and the obligation survives the absence of any particular feeling" (§8); "the guided missile… is the standing reminder that coupling is not yet care" (§1/§9). The closing §10 ("Care has a shape…") earns its cadence. The discipline of the weak/strong layering is the prose carrying real conceptual weight.

**Dead/weak:** §4 reads as a glossary — radius, diameter, depth, curvature, topology defined in sequence, competent but inert. §6's hedging on active inference is sound but boilerplate ("The defensible use is local"). The abstract is a single ~200-word clause-chain that buries its own claims; it should be broken up. Several sentences accrete subordinate clauses past the point of readability.

**Genuine contribution:** a *reorganizing vocabulary*, not a result — moral-psychology findings recast as curvatures of a single cost-field, institutions as engineered care-manifolds, alignment as care-geodesics under an autonomy constraint. That unification is real and the Levinas/corrigibility convergence is its sharpest moment. The paper is honest that it offers organization, not ethics. Its value is as a strong position/theory essay; it is not an empirical contribution and should not be dressed as one.

## Top fixes
1. **Fix the quasi-metric terminology.** Either rename the object precisely (a *directed premetric* / *asymmetric divergence* — something that retains *no* triangle inequality) or, if "quasi-metric" is kept, explicitly state and justify a non-standard usage and stop pairing it with "violates the triangle inequality" as though the two were compatible. This touches the abstract, §3, the §7.1 heading, and the Figure 2 caption. It is the highest-leverage change because it is the paper's load-bearing formal claim.
2. **Re-scope the simulation's rhetoric to match §7's own concession.** The abstract and §7.3 should say the model *demonstrates realizability and internal coherence* of the structures, not that it "makes the claims concrete" or shows "kin selection rendered as geometry." State plainly that asymmetry, triangle-failure, and the kin gradient are stipulated, not derived.
3. **Repair the autonomy operationalization, or flag it.** Add a sentence in §5/§7.2 acknowledging that "option-entropy" is the entropy of the *carer-imposed* move distribution, not B's own choice, and that this is a proxy for — not a model of — the recipient's power to refuse and redirect. As written it conflates carer stochasticity with recipient agency.
4. **Qualify "the price of autonomy is small."** State that 0.028 is a property of the chosen horizon, start state, and ln 2 floor (which still lets the carer exclude the harmful move), not a general feature; show or cite sensitivity to those settings.
5. **Fix the two citations and the abstract wording bug.** Crimston title "circle"→"world"; Singer subtitle → either keep 1981/FSG with "Ethics and Sociobiology" or cite the 2011 Princeton edition; and rewrite "the welfare lost to coercion's failure is large" to refer to abandonment.
6. **Break up the abstract** into 3–4 sentences so its three model-claims are legible, and tighten §4 from a glossary into prose that earns each term with the moral question it answers (as the section already promises but does not deliver).
